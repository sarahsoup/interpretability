HUMAN INTERPRETABILITY STUDY
Hello, and thank you for participating in our study!

We are researchers at the University of Utah, the University of Washington, and Northeastern University. We have developed a software application that measures human empathy, and we’re interested in hearing your thoughts about it.

We would like you to listen to a few minutes of a role-played session between a therapist and a client. Then, we’d like you to rate the counselor’s empathy on a scale of 1-5, where 1 means “the therapist demonstrated little or no empathy for the client”, and 5 means, “the therapist was extremely empathic.”

Next, we’ll show you how our algorithm rated the therapist’s empathy. We’ll also provide additional information about the software, and about the session. We’d like you to spend a few minutes reviewing the session and the rating, to try and get a sense of how well the software works.

Finally, we’ll ask you to complete a brief survey about our system.

The entire study should take about 15-20 minutes, and your answers are confidential. When you are finished, you may leave your contact information if you would like to be included in a drawing for a $50 Amazon gift card.

If you have questions about this study or would like additional information, please contact humaninterpretability@gmail.com.
